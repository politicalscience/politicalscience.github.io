Course List

Introductory Statistical Theory

This is an introduction to statistical thinking and probability for Political Science. This course focuses on building the foundations for the applied methods taught in the subsequent courses.

Prerequisites: None, but an understanding of Algebra would be recommended.


Lesson Topics
Data and Measurement Types of Variables, Operationalisation, Descriptive Statistics
Probability I: Basic Probability Theory Counting, Sets, Probability, Bayes’ Rule, Independence
Probability II: Random Variables Random Variables, Discrete and Continuous Distributions, Probability Density Function, Cumulative Density Function
Probability III: Distributions Expected Value, Variance, Uniform Distribution, Normal Distribution, Binomial Distribution, Joint Distributions.
Samples and Populations Sampling, Inference and Population, Data Visualisation
Statistical Inference Hypothesis Testing, Chi-Square Tests, Difference in Means, Confidence Intervals

Regression Analysis

This course is a rigorous introduction to regression techniques frequently used in the social sciences to find relationships between variables.

Prerequisites: Topics covered in Intro to Statistical Inference. Some working knowledge of R and Maths to the level of Workshop: R Fundamentals and Maths for Political Science is also helpful.


Lesson Topics
Linear I: Continuous Explanatory Variables Simple Linear Regression and Multivariate Linear Regression with Continuous Explanatory Variables.
Linear II: Complex Explanatory Variables Categorical Explanatory Variables, Interaction/Moderating Effect, Non-Linear Relationships.
Linear III: Hypothesis Testing and Assumptions Hypothesis Testing, Residual Diagnostics, Heteroscedasticity, Ourtiers, Adjusted \(r^2\).
Linear IV: Non-Independent Data Hierarchical Data, Multilevel Data, Panel Data, Fixed Effects Models, Pooled Regressions, Random Effects.
Logistic I: Binomial Binomial Logistic Regression, Estimation, Odds Ratios
Logistic II: Applications Fitted Probabilities, Classification, Likelihood Ratio Test
Logistic III: Multinomial and Ordinal Multinomial Logistic Regression, Ordinal Logistic Regression
Regression for Counts Count Outcomes, Poisson Regression, Negative Binomial Regression.

Causal Inference

This course is a rigorous introduction to methods to establish causality, experimental design, and how to make causal inferences in political science.

Prerequisites: Topics covered in Intro to Statistical Inference and Regression Analysis. Some working knowledge of R and Maths to the level of Workshop: R Fundamentals and Maths for Political Science is also helpful.


Lesson Topics
Causal Inference Statistical Inference, Potential Outcomes Framework, Selection Bias
Selection on Observables Control Variables, Assumptions, Estimate of Average Treatment Effect.
Randomised Controlled Trials Randomised Experiments, Estimation, Standard Error, Blocking.
Instrumental Variables Instrumental Variable Framework, Identifying Assumptions, Estimation.
Regression Discontinuity Regression Discontinuity, Estimation, Bandwidth Selection, Validation
Differences in Differences Differences in Differences, Estimation, Threats to Validity, Application
Survey Experiments Surveys, Vignette Experiments, List Experiments, Conjoint Experiments

Game Theory and Formal Modelling

Game Theory uses mathematical modelling of strategic interactions, assuming individuals act rationally, to see model outcomes of strategic competition. This course introduces Game Theory (Formal Modelling), and how these mathematical models can contribute to political science research.

Prerequisites: Working Knowledge of Algebra. Some working knowledge of maths to the level of Maths for Political Science is also helpful, but not required.


Lesson Topics
Essential Definitions Key Definitions, Preferences, Definitions.
Nash Equilibria Prisoner’s Dilemma Game, Dominant Strategy, Solution Concepts, Definition of Nash Equilibrium.
Applications of Nash Equilibria Assurance Game, Cournot Equilibria
Electoral Competition Models Hotelling Model, Downsian Models and Median Voter Theory, Model of Public Finance
Mixed Strategies Mixed Strategy Definition, Mixed strategy Equilibria, Valence Politics Model
Dynamic Games of Complete Information Dynamic/Sequential Games, Extensive Form, Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibria, Legislative Models.
Repeated Games Equilibria of Finitely Repeated Games, Infinitely Repeated Games, Nash Threats Folk Theorem, Minimax Threats Folk Theorem, Collusive Industry Model.
Static Bayesian Games Incomplete Information, Battle of the Sexes, Bayesian Nash Equilibrium, BNE and Mixed Strategy Equilibria.
Dynamic Bayesian Games Dynamic Bayesian Games, Player Beliefs, Restriction of Beliefs, Perfect Bayesian Equilibrium.
Advanced Applied Game Theory Rentseeking, Tullock Paradox, Selection and Incentives in Elections, Other Applications.

Quantitative Text Mining

A lot of modern political data takes the form of text: constitutions, political manifestos, political platforms, and political speeches. This course is a rigorous introduction to the machine learning field of quantitative text analysis, allowing us to quantify texts and documents to better understand textual data.

Prerequisites: Topics covered in Applied Machine Learning, as well as Intro to Statistical Inference and Regression Analysis. Some working knowledge of R and Maths to the level of Workshop: R Fundamentals and Topics in Maths for Political Science is also helpful.


Lesson Topics
Description Statistics and Feature Selection Summary Measures, Document Feature Matrices (DFMs), Bag of Words, N-grams, Trimming
Dictionary Methods and Sentiment Analysis Automated dictionary methods, Applications of dictionaries.
Classification Methods for Text Naive Bayes Classifier, Validation, Applications.
Supervised Scaling Models Wordscores method, Link between Classification and Scaling
Unsupervised Scaling Models Poisson Distributed Methods (Wordfish ans Wordshoal), Correspondence Analysis
Similarity and Clustering Methods Similarity metrics, hierarchical clustering, K-means clustering, weighting methods.
Topic Models Latent Dirichlet Allocation, Structural Topic Models
Word Embeddings Word Embeddings
Intro to Neural Network Models Neural Network Models.

Workshop: R Fundamentals

R is a programming language that is commonly used for statistics and data science, particularly in academia. This section will have some R guides on some fundamental techniques needed for the other courses.

This course is not meant to be a complete and full introduction to R. It is best to view this course as a reference guide after you have the basics of R down. Resources for learning R.

Prerequisites: none.


Lesson Col2
Fundamentals of R How to install and load packages, variable types, assignment and pipe operator, other operators.
Managing Data in R Import data into R, selecting and filtering, adding columns, renaming columns, merging datasets.
Visualizing Data in R Ggplot package, Histograms, boxplots, and scatterplots with best fit lines, pairs and correlations.
Long and Wide Data in R Long and wide data formats, pivot longer, pivot wider.

Resources for Learning Statistics and R-Programming

  • For those who need an introduction to statistics, I recommend the Khan Academy course on AP Stats for a mathematical introduction: https://www.khanacademy.org/math/ap-statistics

  • There is also a terrific free textbook on Statistical learning with applications to R: https://www.statlearning.com

  • BurkeyAcademy provides free online econometrics courses and implementations in R: https://www.burkeyacademy.com/statistics-econometrics

  • For more advanced individuals who want to learn more in-depth about statistics, see this excellent MIT lecture series: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLUl4u3cNGP61ATaGTFcSp7bhogloD2wHP

  • A free resource to learn R with is CodeAcademy: https://www.codecademy.com/enrolled/courses/learn-r

  • For those who have spare cash to spend, there is an excellent R programming course (Data Analysis with R) offered by IBM on Coursera, which teaches R and statistics: https://www.coursera.org/learn/data-analysis-with-r

  • I also highly recommend those who can afford it, to attend the London School of Economic (LSE) Summer School, which has an excellent course - ME314 Introduction to Data Science and Machine Learning. Many of these methods are covered in depth in this course, and the course is run by one of the developers of Quanteda, the main textual-analysis R package.

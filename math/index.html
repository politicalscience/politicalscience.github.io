Maths for Political Science

Homepage

These notes covers some of the key topics in algebra, calculus, and linear algebra needed to understand the methods behind political science.


Prerequisites: Algebra to the level of a typical secondary education.


Chapter 1: Topics in Algebra

Foundations of higher level mathematics and basics commonly used.

Lesson Topics
1.1 Functions Dimensionality, Definition of Functions, Roots, Limits, Continuity
1.2 Exponents and Logarithms Exponent Rules, Logarithm Rules
1.3 Sum and Product Notation Summation Notation, Product Notation


Chapter 2: Single Variable Calculus

Selected topics from Single Variable Calculus, usually equivalent to the US Calculus I & II, or AP Calculus AB & BC. Not all calculus topics are covered (l’hopital’s rule and taylor series for example).

Lesson Topics
2.1 Introduction to Derivatives Definition of Derivative, Calculating Simple Derivatives
2.2 Chain Rule Derivatives Composite Functions, Chain Rule
2.3 Exponent and Log Derivatives Derivatives of Exponential Functions, Derivatives of Logarithms
2.4 Partial Derivatives Partial Derivatives, Application in Regression, Application in Economics
2.5 Indefinite Integrals Indefinite Integrals, Calculating Integrals
2.6 Definite Integrals Riemann Sum and Rieman Integral, Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
2.7 Integrating by Substitution U-Subsitution
2.8 Integrating by Parts Integration by Parts for Indefinite and Definite Integrals


Chapter 3: Optimisation

Using calculus methods to optimise equations - useful in both Statistics (minimising error) and Game Theory (after all, game theory is about payoff/utility maximisation).

Lesson Topics
3.1 Maxima and Minima Slope and Concavity, Critical Points, Global and Local Maxima and Minima
3.2 Unconstrained Optimisation Optimisation in Decision Theory, Optimisation in Linear Regression
3.3 Constrained Optimisation Equality Constraints, Langrangian Function


Chapter 4: Intro to Linear Algebra

Statistics relies quite heavily on Linear Algebra, so it is useful to understand.

Lesson Topics
4.1 Vectors Introduction to Linear Algebra, Vectors and Scalars, Graphical Representation of Vectors
4.2 Vector Algebra Vector Addition, Scalar Multiplication, Dot Product
4.3 Matrices Introduction to Matrices, Types of Matrices
4.4 Matrix Algebra Transpose, Addition and Subtraction, Scalar Mutliplication, Plain Multiplication, Kronecker Product
4.5 Matrix Operators
4.6 Properties
4.7 OLS Regression


Chapter 5: Intro to Multivariate Calculus

Useful for multivariate statistics and Game Theory.

Lesson Topics
5.1 Functions of Several Variables
5.2 Further Partial Derivatives
5.3 Multidimensional Integrals
